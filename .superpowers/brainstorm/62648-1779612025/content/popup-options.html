How they differ

A · Side Drawer (Notebook)

  • Two-pane · folder/note rail always visible, content reads beside it.
  • Page stays visible · ~24% of the editorial page remains in view, dimmed. You can tell where you came from.
  • Best for · scanning multiple notes in sequence without losing nav.
  • Tradeoff · content area is narrower; the rail takes width away from prose.

B · Centered Folio (Paper Card)

  • Single-pane · top tabs for folders, prev/next stepper for notes inside the folder.
  • Page fully dimmed · reads as a deliberate "step out" moment. More focused.
  • Best for · reading one note at a time, slowly. Feels like opening a manila folio.
  • Tradeoff · navigating between folders takes one extra click (tab → step).

My read: Option A is more powerful — you can browse the field notes the way you'd browse them in Obsidian itself. Option B is more elegant for one-at-a-time reading. Pick one (or "do A but borrow the tabs from B," etc.).
