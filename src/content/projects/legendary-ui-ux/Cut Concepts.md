# cut concepts

the graveyard. ideas i wrote down, prototyped in my head, and then declined to ship. each one is recorded with a date, a thumbnail, and the reason it didn't make it. i may come back to some of these.

## negative cursor

**the idea.** instead of visualising where the pointer has been (the [[Concept - Phosphor Touch|phosphor]]), visualise where it has *not* been. on a canvas the size of the page, paint everything dark, then erase the dark wherever the pointer has gone. what's left at the end of a reading session is a silhouette of your inattention — every paragraph you didn't dwell on, every margin you didn't approach.

**why it's beautiful.** it's a quiet rebuke. it's the page asking you what you're not reading. it's also a portrait of you that isn't flattering and isn't supposed to be.

**why i cut it.** redundant with the [[Concept - Phosphor Touch|phosphor]] in the same essay — two complementary inversions of the same gesture, side by side, would have made the page feel like a gallery of cursor tricks rather than a piece of writing. it deserves its own essay. one day.

## echo pages

**the idea.** navigation doesn't replace, it accretes. clicking a link doesn't take you to a new page; it pulls the new page over the old one at low opacity. the previous page is visible underneath, fading by maybe 5% per second. hold ⌘ and you can rewind the stack of where you've been, peeling off translucent layers like a contact sheet.

**why i cut it.** the site is one page. there's nowhere to navigate. echo pages need a corpus. file it for the multi-page version that does not exist.

## sediment

**the idea.** content on long-lived pages sediments. paragraphs you've already read drift downward in the z-axis (subtle blur, slight desaturation). new content rises. months later, the post is a stack with the freshest material on top. you can "excavate" by pulling old content back into focus.

**why i cut it.** the site is read in one sitting. sediment only earns its keep over weeks. it's also one css filter away from being a gimmick. needs more thought.

## breath sync

**the idea.** the [[Concept - Reading Tide|tide]] is already a breath. what if the site could *learn yours*? a microphone (with permission, with consent, with one button) detects the user's breath cadence and the tide synchronises to it. the page would breathe with you.

**why i cut it.** mic permission is a wall most users would (rightly) refuse to climb. also: it's a beautiful idea that is one false-positive away from a horror movie. file under "promising, requires a much larger ethical wrapper."

## polyphonic reading

**the idea.** the same essay, three voices. scroll slowly: voice A. scroll fast: voice B. scroll backward: voice C. the prose visible at any moment is the *weave* of where your reading speed has been.

**why i cut it.** would require writing three full essays braided together. a multi-month project to do well. i wrote one essay in a day and a half.

## the page as instrument

**the idea.** every interaction has a unique tone, not a preset. hover over a letter at velocity v: pitch = f(v). click a paragraph: it speaks one of its sentences at a synthesised pitch derived from its character histogram. an entire page can be "played" by gestures.

**why i cut it.** novel and exciting and one notch too far from the manifesto's quiet register. it would have hijacked the resonance section. instead [[Concept - Resonant Field]] is restrained to a single drone with two modulations. saving "page as instrument" for an instrumental version.

## reading temperature

**the idea.** the page warms (literally, in tone temperature) the longer you stay, and cools when you leave. the colour shift is small but real. coming back to the page after a week, it starts cool and warms with you. it would be a way of greeting.

**why i cut it.** in-session warming was easy; cross-session warming requires storage; and the effect competes with the [[Concept - Reading Tide|tide]]. cut for clarity.

## coexistence cursors

**the idea.** ghosts of previous visitors' pointer paths, played back as you scroll. you are reading with company you cannot see. on a one-html-file site with no server this is impossible — unless the "ghosts" are procedurally generated paths that *resemble* having been left by other readers, in which case they are a fiction with a real social effect.

**why i cut it.** beautiful idea, dishonest implementation. a fake ghost is worse than no ghost. revisit if the site ever gets a backend.
