To your question · "do sub-notes pop up as a set below?"

Yes. Both mockups show it. Click a folder → its notes appear below the folder line. Click a folder again → they collapse back. Click a note → its rendered markdown appears in an inset paper panel directly below the note title. Multiple notes can be open at once (matches Obsidian's read mode).

To your tension · "B looks better, A feels less intrusive"

Three knobs that lower B's intrusiveness:

  • Backdrop opacity · went from 50% to 28%. The page is now visible behind, just slightly hushed. (Try it — the previous mockup had a much heavier veil.)
  • Card width · narrower (520px max vs the 580 before). The page peeks around the card.
  • No blur · pure dim, no defocus. Page stays legible if you glance at it.

Option C resolves the tension differently: takes B's paper feel and accordion behavior, but anchors it to the right edge like A. You get the paper warmth + the partial-page-visible reassurance. Tradeoff: it covers ~60% of the page (more than B's 35%), so it's not strictly "less intrusive" in surface area — but it doesn't float, which is what often reads as intrusive.

Take a look. Tell me: B' (the lighter centered folio) or C (the paper side panel)?
