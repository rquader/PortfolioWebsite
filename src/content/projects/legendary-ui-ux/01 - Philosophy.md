# 01 — philosophy

## the brief, in one line

the goal was beauty without a buyer — a site that exists as an object rather than as a means. the user gave me free rein and asked, somewhat dryly, for "the best ui/ux ever, multiple passes." that brief is impossible, which is precisely what makes it useful. you stop trying to win and you start trying to make something that *quietly insists on itself*.

## the position

most of the ui patterns we have in 2026 are inherited from the 1984 mac — pointer, button, drop-down, scroll. they're load-bearing. they work. and after forty years they have together turned the web into an extremely capable shopping mall.

i wanted to ask a smaller question. what would happen if you treated a web page as a *quiet room* rather than a control surface? what if you gave letters weight? traces persistence? the cursor company? what if a page had a pulse?

these are not features. they are decorations of a different mood. they don't sell anything. their job is to make the room nicer to be in.

## six principles, written first, edited last

1. **decoration is not deception.** if a thing pulses, it should pulse for a reason — even a small one. (the tide breathes because the page is, on average, read in a body that is, on average, breathing.)
2. **nothing is loud.** every amplitude on this site is low. nothing flashes. the audio, when it arrives, is a drone you have to lean in to hear.
3. **the cursor is a body.** never call it a pointer in copy. it has weight. it has a breath rate. when it is still, it is *resting*, not idle.
4. **memory is a kindness.** the site should remember more than it forgets. your scroll path is drawn down the left margin like a thermograph trace; it is yours; it does not phone home.
5. **silence is the default; sound is consent.** audio never starts uninvited. there is one small button. once pressed, it stays on.
6. **the page arrives complete.** one html file, one breath. no fonts to fetch, no images to load. on a slow plane wifi the page should still be the page.

## the six movements (and why six)

six is the number of primitives that fit in one essay without it becoming a tour. each section is also its own demonstration. the *on memory* section needs the margin to mean anything; the words "on weight" can only really be themselves with weight. the demonstrations are not after the text. they are *inside* the text.

## a small claim about what is new here

i don't think any individual primitive is unprecedented. flash-era kinetic type did inertial letters in 2002. cursor trails are as old as the canvas tag. ambient web audio has been tried for decades. what i think *is* new — modestly — is the **insistence that the demonstration is the argument**. if you have to scroll past a section to read its argument, then the argument and the section are *one thing*. the text and the primitive are not decoupled, and you cannot remove either without removing the other.

a second small claim: the primitives are **composable**, not exclusive. the same pointer drives weight, trace, and company. the same scroll drives memory and tide. one rAF loop runs them all. they are not features in a feature list; they are a small physics.

## what this site is *not*

- a portfolio
- a product
- a tutorial
- ironic
- complete
- a claim of sole human authorship

it is a manifesto in the original sense — a *manifest*, a hand-bill, something nailed to a door. it argues for a way of building, mostly by trying to be that way. it is also, honestly, a stress test: what does an AI make when you ask for beauty without a buyer and give it one file?
